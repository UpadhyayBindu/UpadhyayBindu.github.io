Redesigning CARE, an at-home infant warmer

Client: Embrace Innovations

Duration: 6 months

My role

  • Project planning and execution
  • Field immersion
  • Market ready prototype
  • Collaboration with sales, marketing, QA and hospitals
  • User testing in hospitals
  • Technical documentation including product specs and testing plan

Brief

Embrace Innovations is a Stanford-based social enterprise that develops disruptive healthcare technologies focused on reducing infant and maternal deaths in emerging markets. My project was to redesign the infant warmer so that it could meet the performance requirements.

Gathering Requirements

  • Interviewed the sales team to identify frequent complaints from users
  • Performed Root Cause Analysis through testing
  • Went to the users in remote parts of India to understand the use case of the product

Outcome: Identified the causes of heat loss from the product

Ideation

I identified that human error was the important reason for the product not meeting it's requirements. I then redesigned the Baby Bed by drawing inspiration from how mothers wrap their babies. After quick prototyping, I followed this up with co-creation by talking to mothers and nurses.

      Prototyping and User Testing

      I made several prototypes and performed technical tests as well as user tests. I also sourced material from China to make more finer prototypes. I also wrote test plans to enable the QA team to perform testing.

      Result

      Once the final design was ready, I initiated the Engineering Change Request (ECR) . An independent team conducted the final tests and the Engineering Change Order (ECO) was passed.
