Conceptualising a Dashboard for Cardiologists

Client: Do CHANGE EU Project

Team: Bindu, Farzaneh and Kateryna

Duration: 4 weeks

My role

  • Remote interview of doctors
  • Faciliate Google Design Sprint
  • Interactive prototype in Axure RP
  • Coordinate with multiple stakeholders
  • User testing with stakeholders
  • Presentation to the client

Brief

DO Change is an EU funded project. They required a dashboard for the cardiac rehab team to get a quick glimpse of patient history from wearable devices.

Gathering Requirements

    During the duration of the project, we did not have access to doctors. Thus we employed a novel approach to gather requirements by studying the current process that doctors and nurses use to gather patient history. Through this exercise, and by speaking to a cardiologist remotely, we gathered the most important metrics that need to be displayed. 

    Ideation

    I facilitated the Google Design Sprint so that we could narrow down the focus of the project. This also helped us to quickly arrive at a stage where we had three concepts ready to be prototyped. 

      Outcome: Generated an extensive and prioritised list of requirements 

      Outcome: Generated an extensive and prioritised list of requirements 

        Prototyping and User Testing

        We chose Axure RP and d3.js to make an interactive prototype. We built a dashboard which was customizable depending on the end user (doctor, nurse or rehab specialists). We also tested our prototype with two doctors and also our stakeholders who were responsible for the implementation.

        Result

        We presented the dashboard to the stakeholders. They were very impressed with the progress that we made in such a short duration. They liked the way doctors could interact with it quickly to view the relevant patient parameters. This is under development for the clinical trial.
