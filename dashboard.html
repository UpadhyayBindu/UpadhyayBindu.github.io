Conceptualising a Dashboard for Cardiologists

Client: Do CHANGE, EU Project

Team: Bindu, Farzaneh and Kateryna

Duration: 4 weeks

My role

  • Remote interview of doctors
  • Faciliate Google Design Sprint
  • Interactive prototype in Axure RP
  • Coordinate with multiple stakeholders
  • User testing with stakeholders
  • Presentation to the client

Brief

Do CHANGE is an EU funded project whose primary objective is to develop a health ecosystem for integrated disease management of citizens with high blood pressure and patients with ischemic heart disease or heart failure.  For the next clinical trial, the stakeholders of the project required a tool which would enable the cardiac rehab team to get a quick glimpse of patient history from wearable devices used in daily life.

Gathering Requirements

We employed a novel approach to gather requirements by studying the current process that doctors and nurses follow in their routine to gather patient history. Through this exercise, and by conducting a semi structured interview with a cardiologist remotely, we gathered the most important metrics from the wearble devices that the doctors would be interested.  

Ideation

After the initial need gathering, we decided to use the Google Design Sprint method to come up with concepts. Having read the book, I facilitated the Google Design Sprint as I wanted to apply it in a project. This method helped us to define the focus of our part in the project and also to arrive at a stage where we had three concepts ready to be prototyped.  

Objectives for our part of the project:

  • Design a dashboard for cardiologists 
  • Visualize relevant information
  • Provide comprehensive overview

      Prototyping and User Testing

      We chose Axure RP to make an interactive prototype and d3.js to build the data visualisations. We also studied guidelines for visualising health data and this helped us in building additional guidelines for different types of data. We built a dashboard which could be customized depending on the end user (doctor, nurse or rehab specialists). We tested our prototype with two doctors and also our stakeholders who were responsible for the implementation.

      The iterative approach enabled us to quickly make changes based on the feedback and learnings from the user tests.

      Result

      We presented the dashboard to the stakeholders. They were impressed with the progress that we made in such a short duration. They liked the way doctors could interact with it quickly to view the relevant patient parameters. This is under development for the clinical trial.
