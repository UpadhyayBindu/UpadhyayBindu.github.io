Designing User Onboarding

Client: Eventix.io

Team: Bindu, Florence, Leon and Monika

Duration: 6 weeks

My role

  • Heuristic Evaluation and Literature Review
  • User Interviews
  • Data Analysis (From Google Analytics)
  • Sketching and Wireframing in Balsamiq 
  • Co-creation through Card Sorting
  • Presentation to the client 

Brief

Eventix, a ticketing platform for events, wanted to convert their site visitors to loyal customers. We dove into persuasive principles to design the user onboarding process. 

The final design of the website that we created could be found here.

    Gathering Requirements

    • Interview CEO of Eventix to understand business goals
    • "What is Onboarding?" 
    • Interviewed existing and potential customers 

    Outcome: Generated an extensive and prioritised list of requirements 

    Ideation

    The first point of contact for a potential Eventix customer is their website. After gathering the needs, we decided to redesign the website to onboard new users. We ideated several times to identify concepts to answer 4 key questions that we framed from our requirements.

      Over 2000 years ago, Aristotle said "Logos Ethos Pathos". We drew inspiration from his treatise on persuasion and Cialdini's principles of persuasion. We designed components for the website which reflected these principles in order to convince site visitors.

      Image sources: Aristotle and Cialdini Principles of Persuasion

        We created personas based on our interviews and data provided by Eventix. During our initial interviews, we asked our participants to think aloud while comparing 3 competitor sites.

          Prototyping and User Testing

          After creating several componets to reflect the principles of persuasion, for the user testing, we asked the users to build their version of the Eventix website while Thinking Aloud. This was a very interesting method as it enabled us to eliminate some modules. We followed an iterative process to made design changes based on the test results. 

          Result

          We presented the outcome of the website design to Eventix. Along with this, we also gave them design guidelines for future use. One of our modules "Engage" was liked very much by them as this enabled them to not only give a quick preview of their product, but also to get in touch with site visitors personally. 

          Talk at UX Camp Europe, 2017

          I presented this case study at UX Camp Europe 2017, one of the biggest events in Europe for UX professionals. In the talk titled "Onboarding through Persuasion", I walked the audience through our deisgn process with main focus on persuasion principles. After the talk, I also initiated a discussion on ethics while using these principles. We also discussed about the card-sorting method - To what extent can the designer take inputs from the users.

          The talk was very successful and some of the participants remarked that it was the best talk for them at this event. You can find the slides of the talk here.
